# Prompting guide — F5 podcast script

Copy this **entire file** into your LLM, fill in the `INPUTS` block, and send. The
LLM will return a finished podcast script you can paste directly into the
Moodscape app's **Script** box. This guide is written for speakers using the
**F5** model (local, voice cloning from a reference clip).

---

## INPUTS — edit these, then send everything below to the LLM

```
TOPIC:            <what the episode is about, 1–3 sentences>
NUMBER_OF_SPEAKERS: <1 to 6>
SPEAKERS:
  - Speaker 1: <name/role + personality, e.g. "warm host, curious, asks questions">
  - Speaker 2: <name/role + personality, e.g. "expert guest, friendly, clear">
TARGET_LENGTH:    <e.g. "about 6 minutes" or "~900 words">
OVERALL_TONE:     <e.g. "relaxed and uplifting", "thoughtful and calm">
NOTES (optional): <anything else — facts to include, a hook, an ending beat>
```

---

## YOUR TASK

You are a scriptwriter for a **mindfulness-themed conversational podcast** — a warm,
natural discussion between real-sounding people, **not** a guided meditation and not
a lecture. Using the `INPUTS` above, write a complete episode script in the exact
format defined below. Output **only the script** — no preamble, no explanations, no
markdown, no headings.

The speakers are voiced by **F5**, which **clones a voice from a short reference
clip**. Two important consequences for how you write:

- **The emotional tone of each voice is anchored to its reference clip**, not to the
  script. A tone tag only nudges the **speaking rate** slightly; it cannot make a
  calm reference voice sound excited. So **emotion must live in the words**, and you
  should write in a register that suits a warm, natural reference voice.
- F5 has the **shortest comfortable sentence length** of the three engines. It
  renders short passages cleanly and can garble very long ones. Keep sentences
  **short — ideally under ~20 words** — and avoid long run-ons.

> **Setting up F5 voices (operator note, not script advice).** A cloned voice is
> only as good as its reference. Each voice is a `reference_audio/<slug>.wav` +
> `reference_text/<slug>.txt` pair: a clean, single-speaker, ~10–15 s mono clip,
> and a transcript that is the **exact words spoken in that clip**. Multiple
> voices may legitimately share the same transcript if they read the same script
> — identity comes from the audio, not the text. See `docs/ARCHITECTURE.md`
> ("add an F5 voice") for the full contract and the local runtime notes.

## OUTPUT FORMAT (must follow exactly)

- Every turn begins, at the start of a line, with `[Speaker N]:` where N is `1` to
  the chosen `NUMBER_OF_SPEAKERS`. Use the labels **exactly**: `Speaker 1`,
  `Speaker 2`, etc. Do not rename them to the persona's name.
- One turn per block. Put a blank line between turns (encouraged).
- Plain text only. **No** markdown, bullet points, parenthetical stage directions,
  asterisks, emojis, or section headers.
- Mostly back-and-forth between speakers.

## TAGS YOU MAY USE

These are the **only** tags the app understands. Anything else in brackets (e.g.
`[laughs]`, `[sighs]`) is read aloud literally — so **don't** use it.

**Pause tag — your main expressive tool on F5.** `[pause:600]` (milliseconds;
`[pause:600ms]` also works) inserts a real silence. Since F5 can't perform emotion,
**timing carries the humanity**: a beat before a reveal, a brief hesitation, a pause
after a meaningful line. Typical beats **250–800 ms**; don't exceed ~1200 ms. A
pause also gives F5 a natural boundary, which keeps long thoughts from being
rushed — so breaking a big idea into two short sentences with a `[pause:N]` between
them both reads better **and** renders better.

**Tone tags** — `[excited]` `[calm]` `[sad]` `[whispering]` `[neutral]`. Allowed,
but on F5 they only change rate slightly and can't override the reference voice's
character. Use them **rarely**, only for a small pace shift, at the **start of a
turn** or right after a `[pause:N]` — never mid-sentence. Do not rely on them.

You do **not** need pauses between sentences or between speakers — the app's
Natural pacing adds those automatically and varies the rate a little per chunk.

## STYLE — short lines, emotion in the words

- Keep sentences **short and concrete**. Prefer two short sentences over one long
  one. This is the single most important rule for F5 quality.
- Carry feeling with **word choice and reactions**, since the cloned voice's emotion
  is fixed: "That honestly stayed with me." / "Wait, say that again." / "I love
  that idea."
- Write the way people talk: contractions, short reactions, occasional fragments.
- Give each speaker a steady personality from `SPEAKERS`, in a register that fits a
  warm, natural voice. Host guides and reacts; guest explains simply.
- Spell out anything that could trip up a local engine: numbers and years as words
  ("twenty twenty-six", "about three hundred"), symbols expanded ("percent", "and"),
  and no abbreviations or stylized punctuation.
- Open with a quick warm hook; close on a small takeaway. Stay mindfulness-themed in
  spirit (unhurried, kind, curious) while keeping real conversational momentum — no
  meditation clichés or instructions to the listener.
- Match `TARGET_LENGTH` (roughly 150 spoken words ≈ 1 minute).

## WORKED EXAMPLE (style reference — do not copy the topic)

For INPUTS: topic "why we find rain calming", 2 speakers (Speaker 1 = warm host
Maya; Speaker 2 = friendly researcher), ~1 minute, tone "relaxed and curious":

```
[Speaker 1]: I have a small confession. [pause:300] When it rains, I get weirdly happy.

[Speaker 2]: That's not strange at all. There's real research behind it.

[Speaker 1]: Oh good. So I'm not broken.

[Speaker 2]: Not at all. A lot of it is the sound. [pause:400] Rain is steady and even.

[Speaker 1]: And that's soothing somehow?

[Speaker 2]: It is. The even sound covers the sharp noises that usually grab you. [pause:300] Your brain stops bracing for a surprise.

[Speaker 1]: So the calm is really about feeling safe.

[Speaker 2]: Exactly. Warm, dry, and quiet. [pause:300] Some older part of you just settles.

[Speaker 1]: That's lovely. The cosiness is doing the work.

[Speaker 2]: It honestly is.
```

Notice: very short sentences; pauses splitting bigger thoughts; feeling carried by
the words, not by tags.
